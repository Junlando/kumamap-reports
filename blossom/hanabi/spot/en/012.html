Ashikaga Fireworks Festival

📅 Aug 1 📍 Tochigi
A century-old tradition dating back to the Meiji era, featuring 20,000 dazzling fireworks along the Watarase River.

Founded in 1903 (Meiji 36), this festival boasts a prestigious history of over a century. It is held on the first Saturday of August at the riverside below Tanaka Bridge on the Watarase River in Ashikaga City, Tochigi Prefecture, showcasing an impressive scale of 20,000 fireworks, making it one of the most famous events in northern Kanto.

The festival features creative competitive fireworks crafted by master pyrotechnicians from across Japan, large continuous rapid-fire displays, and a breathtaking 'aerial waterfall' that stretches hundreds of meters, showering golden sparks. On the night of the event, hundreds of traditional Japanese festival stalls line both sides of the riverbank.

Visitors can take the JR Ryomo Line to 'Ashikaga Station' and reach the riverside venue in just 5 minutes on foot; alternatively, they can take the Tobu Isesaki Line to 'Ashikagashi Station' and walk about 10 minutes, making it very accessible for travelers.
